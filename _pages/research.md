## [Georgia Institute of Technology](https://www.gatech.edu/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"}

**Research Assistant, Advisor: Prof. [Munmun De Choudhury](https://www.cc.gatech.edu/people/munmun-de-choudhury){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"}**  
Aug. 2025 - Present

I work on AI agents and clinical mental healthcare, focusing on human-AI interaction frameworks that adapt clinical support structures into transparent, role-bounded conversational agent design.

- Co-designed clinician focus groups and IRB question sets to evaluate the coherence and acceptability of the framework in real clinical decision-making scenarios.
- Developed questioning and mapping protocols that preserve clinicians' own language while assessing framework behavior.
- Built backend services and benchmarked vLLM on Azure under high-concurrency workloads to evaluate scalability and system performance.

## [UC San Diego](https://ucsd.edu/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"}

**Research Assistant, Advisor: Prof. [Pengtao Xie](https://pengtaoxie.github.io/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"}**  
Mar. 2025 - Present

I work on MetaboliteChat, a unified multimodal large language model for interactive metabolite analysis and functional insight generation.

- Built a metabolic data pipeline to collect metabolite data and generate large-scale QA pairs for structure-aware dialogue.
- Used DeepSeek-8B summarization to reduce long-context truncation and improve input efficiency.
- Designed a hybrid multimodal encoder using GNNs for molecular graphs and CNNs for images, projecting embeddings into the LLaMA/Vicuna-13B input space.
- Fine-tuned LLaMA/Vicuna-13B with adapters and evaluated semantic alignment, BLEU, ROUGE, and semantic similarity against strong baselines.

## [Cornell University](https://www.cornell.edu/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"}

**Research Assistant, Advisors: Prof. [Mert Sabuncu](https://www.duffield.cornell.edu/people/mert-sabuncu/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"} and [Ruining Deng](https://ddrrnn123.github.io/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"}**  
Mar. 2025 - Present

I work on vision-language models for fine-grained renal pathology, with a focus on diseased glomerulus classification, cross-modal understanding, and clinically interpretable model behavior.

- Built an automated pipeline to curate approximately 70K medical image-text pairs for classification fine-tuning.
- Used Qwen2.5-VL for automated quality control, glomerulus image classification, and caption rewriting.
- Evaluated CLIP, PLIP, CONCH, GigaPath, and TITAN on confidential glomerulus datasets.
- Fine-tuned CLIP and CoCa models with strategies including vanilla fine-tuning, LoRA, adapters, and classifier-head tuning.
- Designed zero-shot and few-shot experiments to analyze generalization, multimodal alignment, negative sample separability, and clinically meaningful embeddings.

## [New York University](https://www.nyu.edu/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"}

**Research Assistant, Advisor: Prof. [Juliana Freire](https://engineering.nyu.edu/faculty/juliana-freire){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"}**  
Jan. 2025 - Present

I work on public data quality, developing taxonomies and LLM-assisted pipelines for detecting and explaining errors in real-world open datasets.

- Surveyed public data quality issues including missing documentation, schema violations, semantic inconsistencies, and logical conflicts.
- Designed Qwen3-14B-assisted error detection pipelines that generate interpretable data quality reports.
- Built tools to process and normalize 3,000+ NYC OpenData datasets, extracting metadata from JSON and Excel attachments.
- Contributed to an S-BERT-based pipeline for clustering semantically similar columns and propagating LLM-assigned semantic types.
